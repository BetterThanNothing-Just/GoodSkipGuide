Anorexia

It's summer again and it's dressing for fun
and fat once again's not in fashion
I'll lose it this year
for my health and my pride
(and perhaps a side order of passion)

The money I've saved by not buying food
helps pay for the dieting books
it's calorie counting
and "no more for me"
(I'll soon have those delicate looks)

I'm doing my bit to look after myself
I'm eating the right sort of stuff
I don't eat too much
of the the fatty and fun
(well in fact I just don't eat enough)

It's going ok, looking after the shape
so the body looks good on the beach
cholesterol's down
much less strain on the heart
(and the svelte look seems just within reach)

The hunger's departed, the fats dropping off
and the craving's a thing of the past
the rate of its going
has left me quite stunned
(maybe that's why they call it a fast)

It's rather depressing, I can't seem to cope
I'm at the emotional end of my tether
I feel quite guilty
just biting my nails
(and I've stopped solid food altogether)

Now I'm wasted and scrawny, I'm just skin and bone
and my appetite's faded and grey
it's become quite obsessive
am I dying of health?
(Oh! no, surely this can't be the way...)
